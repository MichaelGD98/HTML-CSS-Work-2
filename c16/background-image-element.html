Magnolia

Magnolia grandiflora. Commonly known as the Southern Magnolia or bull bay. is a tree of the family Magnoliaceae native to the southeastern United States. From coastal Virginia south to central Florida and west to eastern texas and Oklahoma. Reaching 27.5 m (90 feet) in height, it is a large striking evergreen tree with large dark green leaves and a large white fragrant flowers. Widely cultivated around the world, over a hundred cultivators have been bred and marketed commercially.
